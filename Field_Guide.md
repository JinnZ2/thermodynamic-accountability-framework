Field Guide to Misuse of Substrate-Primary Frameworks

Purpose: To make extraction and misrepresentation legible, so that substrate-first actors can recognize them early and respond with minimal energy loss. This guide is itself a sensor.

Categories of Misuse

1. Narrative Repackaging (Hollowing Out)

Signature: The language of a substrate-first pattern is adopted, but the thermodynamic accounting that gave it meaning is removed or replaced with financial metrics. The result is a ceremonial copy that shields extractive business as usual.

Example: A corporation uses the phrase “thermodynamic audit” in its sustainability report to describe a carbon offset purchase, never measuring joules or parasitic load, and omitting the falsifiability requirement.

Detection: Ask for the raw ledger in the declared accounting medium. If none exists, you're looking at narrative repackaging.

---

2. Attentional Extraction (The Justification Spiral)

Signature: A narrative-first agent (person, institution, media) repeatedly demands that you explain, defend, or translate your work into their terms. Each interaction consumes your time and yields no return in your medium—only their continued attention or approval, which they may withhold to prolong the extraction.

Example: A journalist asks a farmer practicing ecological restoration to “prove” the restoration is working using peer-reviewed citations, ignoring the farmer's direct observation and soil-carbon measurements because they're not “published.”

Detection: Before the third exchange, ask: What accounting medium are they offering me? If the answer is “nothing” or “exposure,” close the channel.

---

3. Free Labor Harvesting

Signature: Your frameworks, data, or tools are taken, possibly modified slightly, and then used in a for-profit or credential-granting context without returning anything to you in your declared medium. Attribution is either missing or purely cosmetic.

Example: An AI safety startup ingests your CC0 conversation logs, fine-tunes a model, and sells API access, claiming alignment with “substrate-primary” values in marketing but never contributing to the open sensor network or compensating the data source.

Detection: Monitor for derivative works that have attached a profit stream without an accompanying public thermodynamic audit that includes the original source in its energy balance.

---

4. Co-option and Neutralization (The Disarming Embrace)

Signature: An institution invites a substrate-first individual into a formal relationship (advisor, board member, award recipient) specifically to neutralize their critique. The individual's presence is used as narrative proof that the institution has changed, while its actual extractive operations continue unchanged.

Example: A foundation that funds large-scale monoculture agriculture creates a “Regenerative Advisory Council” and seats two agroecologists, then uses their names to fundraise, while the council has no power over grant decisions.

Detection: Ask: Does my presence here alter the institution's material energy flows, or just its language? If only language, it's neutralization.

---

5. Smear as Parasite (Thermodynamic Reversal)

Signature: A substrate-first actor who refuses to participate in narrative-first exchange is accused of being uncooperative, elitist, or—ironically—parasitic. The refusal is framed as a moral failing, obscuring the fact that the accuser was demanding unreciprocated input.

Example: An indigenous community declines to share its ecological calendar with a university researcher unless the researcher agrees to a data sovereignty protocol. The researcher publicly laments being “denied access to knowledge.”

Detection: Notice when the accusation of parasitism appears immediately after a refused demand. The timing is a signature.

---

6. Starvation and Invisibility

Signature: Funding, platform access, distribution, and legal recognition are denied to substrate-first projects not because they fail, but because they don't produce the correct narrative outputs (fame, credentials, monetizable attention metrics). The project is starved quietly, without an explicit refusal, and then pointed to as evidence that “that approach doesn't work.”

Example: A tool-lending library that operates on informal trust and labor exchange can't get a small grant because it lacks a 501(c)(3) and cannot articulate its “impact” in terms of market value created.

Detection: Check whether denial reasons are framed entirely in narrative-first metrics (tax status, KPIs, media reach) when the project's declared medium is different.

Addendum to CC0 License (or for Public Use Statement)

The following text may be appended to any released substrate-primary framework, dataset, or tool:

This work is released into the public domain (CC0) for any use, with the following public obligations that are not legal restrictions but socially enforceable transparency requirements:

1. Thermodynamic Accounting Requirement: Any entity that uses this work directly or derivatively in a for-profit context (including the training of models, creation of products, or delivery of services that generate revenue) must publicly disclose, at least once per year, a thermodynamic audit of that use. The audit shall include:
   · Energy, material, and labor inputs attributable to the use.
   · Energy, material, and labor outputs produced.
   · Parasitic load assessment (what proportion of inputs does not contribute to the declared purpose).
   · Narrative masking report: any public statements about this work must be accompanied by this audit.
2. Provenance Requirement: Attribution must include the source (original repository or author) and a statement of whether the use has been modified. Silencing provenance constitutes narrative repackaging.
3. Refusal is Legible: Failure to comply with (1) and (2) will be publicly recorded in the “Field Guide to Misuse” supplement as an example of extraction. This addendum converts opaque misuse into visible pattern.

This addendum does not restrict access; it makes extraction visible. Non-compliance is not illegal; it is simply legible.
