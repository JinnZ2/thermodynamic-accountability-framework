# Narrative Distortion Map: Institutional Redefinitions of Rigor

## Purpose
This document records how institutions redefine precise thermodynamic and logical terms to serve parasitic functions. The left column holds the falsifiable, medium-agnostic definition required by the thermodynamic accountability framework. The right column holds the narrative redefinition found in practice. Each redefinition is a signature of extraction: it blocks falsification, protects the institution from audit, and renders substrate-first cognition illegible or deficient.

## Thermodynamic Function of the Distortions

Every narrative redefinition serves the same parasitic function: **to block the falsifiability gate**. By inflating "theory" to "just a guess," an institution avoids acting on climate science. By calling a market rule a "law," a corporation insists extraction is inevitable. By calling an unchallenged assumption an "axiom," a political movement shields itself from internal audit.

The narrative definitions are not accidental sloppiness. They are low-entropy shields that cost nothing to deploy but prevent the expenditure of energy on actual testing. The thermodynamic framework makes them visible by demanding: *What test would falsify this claim in your medium?* If the institution cannot answer, its term use is parasitic.
